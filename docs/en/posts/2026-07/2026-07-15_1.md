# Why Good People Get Stepped On

There is something strange about the way the world works. The nastier a person is, the less people dare to push them around. But if you happen to be a good person, everyone seems willing to step on you. It is not that people suddenly cannot tell right from wrong. When they do their calculations, though, fairness is rarely the first thing they count. The cost is.

An overbearing person speaks harshly, wears a hard expression, and may turn the smallest slight into trouble. People learn to give them room. A kind person is different. You are accommodating, you understand, you do not want to make things awkward. Others begin treating your restraint as something owed to them. Give someone a favor once, and they do not remember the goodwill; they remember that the road is open. The second and third test soon become a habit.

That does not mean one should learn to be cruel, trading an open heart for a set of teeth. Cruel people usually earn avoidance, not respect. But when a good person cannot bring themselves to say no, kindness starts to look like an unpriced thing that anyone may take a little from.

Help when help is called for. Draw a hard line when it is time. Say things plainly and close the door without feeling guilty. People with a sense of proportion will not leave because you have boundaries. Those always hoping to step on you have little reason to keep around. Let people know your hand can reach out, but it is not there for everyone to put a foot on.
