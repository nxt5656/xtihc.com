# Making Friends Plentiful, and That Silent Bill

The founding leader once uttered an incredibly insightful maxim, one that captures the raw essence of political warfare: "What is politics? Politics is about making as many friends as possible, and making as few enemies as possible."

Decades have passed, but if you look closely at society today, you'll realize this baseline code has not aged a bit. On the contrary, those in power are executing it with ruthless perfection.

Except, their way of "making friends" today isn't about forming alliances or building ideological coalitions. It's much simpler and more transactional—**they are using the massive state budget, national credit, and comprehensive privilege nets to shove millions directly into the pockets of the state system, turning them into screws on the massive state apparatus. These are the people on the public payroll.**

### The "Loyal Allies" in the Massive Payroll Reservoir

According to recent estimates by top-tier academic teams, the total public payroll (broadly defined to include registered civil servants, public institution staff, auxiliary forces, temporary contractors, and retirees drawing state-funded pensions) has ballooned to an astronomical **68.46 million people**. If you include the shadow personnel dependent on state-owned enterprises (SOEs) and quasi-fiscal allocations, this figure easily breaks **80 million**.

What does that actually mean?

It means that roughly **one out of every twenty ordinary citizens is feeding someone on the public payroll**. That is an officer-to-citizen ratio of 1:20.

These 80 million people are not just lines of data in a census bureau's ledger. In the underlying game of politics, they form an impenetrable fortress for the regime. These millions, along with their families, have their social status, comfortable lifestyles, and tomorrow's dinner completely bound to the uninterrupted survival and stability of this giant state machine.

Whenever controversial policies are pushed or power acts with total arbitrariness, this army of vested interests—from powerful directors and bureau chiefs down to municipal grid workers, urban enforcement officers (Chengguan), auxiliary police, and retired cadres drawing stable pensions—instinctively and actively rises to defend the absolute authority of the system. Protecting the machine is, after all, protecting their own iron rice bowl.

They are the "friends" of the state, fed mouth-by-mouth with the blood and sweat of ordinary taxpayers, and they are friends who will never defect.

### The Brutal 25-Fold Pension Gap

To keep this massive army of "friends" happy and loyal, the allocation of state resources exhibits a suffocating degree of favoritism.

Nowhere is this difference more brutally exposed than in the pension system.

Let’s look at two stark sets of official estimates:

First, those within the state's inner circle. Retired civil servants and public sector employees draw an average pension of **6,000 to 6,241 RMB per month**, accompanied by high-tier supplementary corporate annuities and near-total public medical reimbursements.

Second, the silent outsiders. For the rural elderly and farmers—who make up the vast majority of the aging population—the minimum national base pension was only recently raised in 2024 to a measly **123 RMB per month**. Even when including minor local subsidies and negligible individual accounts, the actual national average they receive is a meager **240 RMB per month** (about $33 USD).

**That is an absolute chasm of 25-fold! 6,200 RMB versus 240 RMB.**

Why is there such an absurd divide? Is the state budget truly broke?
If you look at the state subsidies per capita, the truth becomes even more sarcastic. Official data reveals that the state budget subsidizes retired public sector employees at an average of **2,265 RMB per month per capita**, while subsidizing ordinary rural residents at a stingy **183 RMB per month per capita**. That is a 12-fold disparity in state-sponsored favoritism.

The millions of farmers who handed over their grain to the state for decades, tightening their belts to lay the foundations of industrialization, receive a pension in their twilight years that cannot even buy two bottles of cooking oil. Meanwhile, retired public officials, who spent their lives drinking tea and stamping papers without producing any tangible economic value, draw pensions 25 times larger, enabling them to spend their retirements in elegant comfort.

This is the most honest distribution of spoils between "friends" and "outsiders." The peasants are scattered and silent. Even if they are miserable, their lack of organization means they pose zero threat to power. But if the public-payroll "friends" have their benefits cut, the gears of the stability-preserving machine might instantly grind to a halt.

### The Silence of the Taxpayers and the Playground of Power

This absurd game continues to run day after day not just because of the state's endless pampering of its "friends," but because those outside the system have grown accustomed to silence.

There is a highly dangerous, cynical mindset among the public: as long as the blade hasn't touched my neck, as long as I can still put food on my own table, I will close my eyes to the rampant injustice and systemic theft.

But history has proven time and again: **when ordinary people use silence to protect themselves, they are merely using their own flesh to indulge and feed the very monster that is devouring them.**

You think the low pension of a farmer has nothing to do with you, but the result is that your own social security contributions skyrocket every year while your future pension faces the threat of devaluation or default. You think the ballooning public payroll is none of your business, but the government, needing to feed those 80 million mouths, begins inventing new taxes, launching arbitrary fire-safety inspections, and issuing massive fines, ultimately pushing the wave of bankruptcies right to the doorsteps of private business owners and workers.

When ordinary people in a society spend their lives scrambling for crumbs and choosing to be silent bystanders in the face of privilege, they are effectively granting power the blank check to loot their own resources.

The underlying game of politics goes on. In this grand feast of spoils, the well-fed "friends" are laughing and dining at the table, while the silent, ordinary taxpayers, who are paying the bill, are being callously devoured underneath it.
